What is Contamination? Contamination is an unintended presence, or introduction, of contaminants into food or an environment. It is also the presence of an infectious agent on a body surface; also on or in clothes, bedding, toys, surgical instruments or dressings, or other inanimate articles or substances including water, milk, and food, or that infectious agent itself. Furhthermoe, contamination can be the presence of particles, chemicals, and other undesirable substances, such as on or in a process tool, in a process liquid, or in a cleanroom environment; it is the action or state of making or being made impure by polluting or poisoning.


Examples of Contamination

Some examples of contamination are chemical fertilizers and pesticides in food, fly ash in landfills, coal ash in the air, lead in vapes, fungus and bacteria in vapes, cigarette smoke within the environment, oil wihtin the ocean, and harmful smog from factories, vehicles and any other gas-powered appliances.
